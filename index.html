About Me

I am a PhD researcher in nutritional epidemiology and global health with a strong background in data science. My work focuses on quantifying global sugar-sweetened beverage consumption, estimating cardiometabolic disease burden attributable to SSB intake, and modeling cardiovascular diseases and their associated risk factors. I have led large-scale modeling and meta-analytic research using advanced analytical methods to produce policy-relevant evidence, which I have disseminated through publications in high-impact journals with the ultimate goal of improving global health outcomes.

I currently serve as a Postdoctoral Scholar at the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), contributing to the Global Burden of Disease Study.
