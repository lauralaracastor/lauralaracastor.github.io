About Me

Nutritional epidemiologist specializing in global cardiometabolic disease and attributable risk factors.

My work focuses on large-scale and policy-relevant research using advanced quantitative methods. In my current role as a postdoctoral scholar, I contribute to the estimation of disease burdens for the Global Burden of Disease (GBD) study and lead meta-analytical work quantifying the dose-response relationship between systolic blood pressure and heart failure.

I earned my PhD in Nutritional Epidemiology and Data Science from the Friedman School of Nutrition Science and Policy at Tufts University. My doctoral research examined global consumption of sugar-sweetened beverages (SSBs) and quantified their attributable cardiometabolic burden globally and across regions and countries. I produced estimates stratified by age, sex, education level, and area of residence, and conducted cost-effectiveness analyses evaluating the impact of SSB taxation on consumption and disease burden. This work was awarded a pre-doctoral fellowship from the American Heart Association and published in high-impact journals including Nature Medicine and BMJ. It was also recognized by the American Society for Nutrition with the George Bray Obesity Research Student Award and Emerging Leaders Poster Competition, and featured in major media outlets such as the New York Times and National Public Radio.

I also hold a master’s degree in Nutrition and Metabolism from Boston University School of Medicine and a bachelor’s degree in Nutritional Sciences from Universidad de las Américas Puebla, Mexico. I worked as a research assistant at the National Institute of Public Health in Mexico, contributing to projects focused on improving the detection and care of cardiometabolic diseases and their complications.

My work is driven by a commitment to rigorous, data-driven research that informs effective public health policies. I aim to continue advancing epidemiological and quantitative methods, contributing to research that improves population health globally.
